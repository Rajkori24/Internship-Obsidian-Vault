## Sprint 2 Reflection

Sprint 2 was a strong learning experience in understanding how execution discipline impacts delivery. While we maintained consistency in daily standups and improved task visibility, we also faced a few challenges related to unclear requirements and dependency management.

Using the Start–Stop–Continue framework, here is my structured reflection:

---

## 🟢 START

- **Ensure every Jira story has clear acceptance criteria before development begins.**  
    This will reduce ambiguity and prevent rework during testing.
    
- **Conduct a short alignment discussion before starting complex tasks (such as API/SMS features).**  
    A quick 5-minute sync can prevent misunderstandings and technical delays.
    

---

## 🔴 STOP

- **Starting work without confirming external dependencies.**  
    In Sprint 2, SMS integration was delayed because API readiness was not fully validated.
    
- **Waiting too long for stakeholder clarification.**  
    If no response is received within 24 hours for important queries, proactive follow-up should be done.
    

---

## 🔵 CONTINUE

- **Daily standups at 9 AM.**  
    This improved team alignment and helped surface blockers early.
    
- **Proper bug logging in Jira with screenshots and clear reproduction steps.**  
    This made the QA process smoother and more structured.

# 🎯 Action Item for Sprint 3

## What will change:

We will introduce a simple **Story Readiness Checklist** before moving any task to “In Progress.”

Each story must include:

1. Clear acceptance criteria
    
2. Confirmed dependencies (API, design assets, environment access)
    
3. Team effort estimation
    

---

## Why this will help:

Most Sprint 2 delays occurred due to unclear requirements or missing confirmations. This checklist will reduce mid-sprint blockers, improve planning accuracy, and increase delivery confidence.

---

## Ownership

As an intern supporting project coordination, I will ensure this checklist is reviewed during Sprint Planning and backlog refinement discussions.

---

# ✅ Conclusion

Sprint 2 reinforced the importance of preparation before execution. Even small gaps in clarity or dependency validation can create delays later in the sprint.

By implementing the Story Readiness Checklist in Sprint 3, the team can move forward with better structure, fewer blockers, and stronger predictability.

This retrospective has helped me understand how continuous improvement directly impacts team efficiency and delivery quality.
