## 🗣 Assignment 11 – Mini Retrospective (Speaking Version)

Good morning everyone,

For Sprint 2, I conducted a mini retrospective using the **Start–Stop–Continue** framework to evaluate what worked well and what we can improve.

### First – What went well (Continue)

We maintained **consistent daily standups at 9 AM**, which improved visibility and accountability.  
Also, bug logging in Jira was detailed with screenshots and proper steps, which made QA smoother.

### Second – What we should Stop

We started some tasks without fully confirming external dependencies, especially during SMS integration. This caused delays.  
Also, we waited too long for stakeholder clarification instead of following up quickly.

### Third – What we should Start

We should ensure every story has **clear acceptance criteria before development begins**.  
And for complex tasks like API or SMS integration, we should do a short 5-minute alignment discussion before starting.

---

## 🎯 Main Action for Sprint 3

The key improvement I’m proposing is introducing a **Story Readiness Checklist**.

Before moving any task to “In Progress,” we will confirm:

- Clear acceptance criteria
    
- Dependencies validated
    
- Effort estimated
    

This will reduce mid-sprint blockers and improve sprint predictability.

### Closing Statement

Sprint 2 taught me that small preparation gaps can create bigger execution delays.
