For this assignment, I treated the WhatsApp integration as a formal change request rather than casually adding it to the sprint. I documented it properly, assigned a change ID, and aligned it with business objectives.

Then I performed a structured impact analysis across four key constraints: scope, time, cost, and risk.

The feature increases scope by 28% — from 18 to 23 story points — which is significant for an active sprint.  
It would delay the launch by 7–10 days due to API integration, backend changes, frontend updates, testing, and buffer time.  
It also adds ₹30–35k in development and QA costs, plus potential recurring API expenses.  
From a risk perspective, it introduces dependency on the WhatsApp Business API, approval delays, and possible team overload.

Instead of making a reactive decision, I created three strategic options: approve and delay launch, defer to Phase 2, or swap it with an existing feature to balance scope.

Considering the business priority — protecting the Feb 28 launch — I recommended deferring it to Phase 2. This keeps the timeline stable, controls risk, and maintains stakeholder confidence.

Overall, the assignment demonstrates structured change management, trade-off analysis, and business-aligned decision-making.
